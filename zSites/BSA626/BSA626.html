FUR/HAIR part 3

OH MY GOSH!

What a painful learning experience I have had over the past 2 days. I have been constantly hitting my head against this xGen simulation not being able to batch render how I want.

But alas, I have overcome the issue and am all the better for it. It feels like I have just been to the brain gym and done a few sets too many.

So the background story behind this all started when I have gone back a few steps to rework my bashful project. I was feeling like my project was not staying true to the course I had set for myself at the beginning, as I slowly changed my project's view because I was having difficulties getting the fur to work how I wanted it to.

Around the time just before the holiday I had begun noticing that my learning had been slowing down and due to this I was also beginning to not enjoy the course as much, So I made a choice when I came back after the holiday to challenge myself to get over this hurdle and tackle the problem at hand instead of going around it.

So to bring things closer to where we are now. About two days ago I started with the xGen process from where I had left off previously, it was a little confusing to begin with after not touching it for so long. But after a few attempts I was able to groom my models how I liked.

One of the first issues I was having from time to time was that my renders were not showing up in the Arnold render view, This was simple enough to fix, I simply had to save my scene before rendering it and that would normally fix the problem.

The next issue I ran into wass the render was not coming out with the fur if I batch rendered it. I was a little confused as to why it comes out fine in the render, but not in a batch Render, arn’t they the same thing, just multiple times?

Turns out that the Renderer Arnold is a completely separate piece of software, so the difference being, when you render the scene in maya, Arnold render view is able to get all the data locally from the Maya document. Compared to when you send the data to the Arnold externally, it does not have the computed information of the Hair/Fur. This is where Caching (pre calculating) and xGen’s inbuilt patch to Batch Render comes into play.

After kinda understanding this a little better, I was able to Export Patches to Batch Render, or in other words, send the data of the Hair Description to Arnold in a packet of data. So I hit batch render and I had a single frame of my fur rendered Externally from Maya and into Arnold on its own! WOOHOO!

My time of troubleshooting for the first day was over. I had gone home and slept it off. Funny enough, that night I woke up in the middle of the night with my mind thinking about batch rendering an animation. Of the xGen system. So! Now day two of trouble shooting and getting things to work. Remember at this stage none of this really made sense and I was continually having to go back and write down what it was exactly that I was trying to accomplish. Sometimes the questions had easy answers and other times I couldn’t think straight.

About 50 Batch renders in and still no results like I was looking for. I took this approach.

Can I Batch render a single frame of fur. Can I Rotate my geo and render a different frame of fur position. Can I Add dynamic curves and see them working in Maya Can I see the dynamic curves out of Batch Render

I found through this process, I was able to trouble shoot the right things at the right stages. And finally I had a result! I rendered out a simulated version of my xGen Groom!

I came home and tried it on my personal pc.

IT DIDN'T WORK!?

Until later I found that there was a space in my files directory. Which was the culprit of 4 hours or trial and error.

But I have it working now and so much better for all the troubles.

Time to build and groom my character how I want now, then export him out in an animation.

Yay me!

Introduction


For this project I will build a high quality intro cinematic for a game called "bashful".
My focus for this project is to create the highest quality product possible for my current skill level.
The final outcome should be clean and crisp renders with smooth and silky animations.

The mechanics I have invisioned for this game are of the 3D platformer genre, similar to "Crash Bandicoot". Our main character will have the ability to burst into a rageful "power up" form once enough points have been collected.
Making it easier for him to destroy objects and enemies to collect points.

Showing these elements in the cinematic are going to be my main targets to hit.

This inspiration for this game came from a few sources, the music video "The Verve - Bitter Sweet Symphony" the game "Burnout","Crash Bandicoot" and from "Elmo"

bashful

Opening shot: mid-shot. Frame is pitch black, nothing is to be seen. (stage lights sound effect starts) white Spotlight lights center of frame with our character being blinded by the light (like the Mr Bean intro). Reacts like a possum in the headlights of a car. (the sound of more stage lights begin to click, like the batcave scene. *see reference board*) bright white LED panels light the entire room, which reveals nothing much but a blank room and the character.

Characters body language and facial expressions portray a shy/timid creature which does not like being “in the spotlight”. The character is confused about where it is and what is going on.

The character spots something off screen which the audience cannot see. A 3D computer cursor floats into the shot from right to left.

Character is curious as to what this strange thing is. Cautiously looking at the cursor, the cursor suddenly floats around the back of the character and clicks on its bottom. Character jumps and reacts with a slight hop. The character is still quite unsure about what just happened, but a little more cautiously this time looks towards the floating cursor.

As the cursor slowly starts to move again, the Character tries to slowly follow around with the cursor hoping not to be caught off guard like last time. Suddenly the mouse quickly whips around the back of the character and clicks on its bottom again. With another jump from the character.

Now the Character has some idea of what is going on and is beginning to become upset at the situation (shown both with body and face language). The audience should also get a clue about what is going on.

So now the duet has begun, the cursor starts to slowly “prowl” around the character. Almost like a western movie standoff. The cursor’s movement is too fast for our character and begins to click him over and over again, almost toying with him,

The Character does not like this one bit. The cursor starts to get really cheeky and starts to laugh (through body language), Suddenly there is a change in lighting as the character begins to growl, followed by shaking of his fur, followed by an angry expression and growing of his body size.

The camera slowly zooms out to show the size comparison between the mouse and character now. The character mammoths the size of the small cursor.

Our character has a big grin come across his face as he twists his body in anticipation of a big release of energy. *The cursor gulps*.

The Character unwinds and begins a devastating spin attack, as the floor begins to tear towards the center and the cursor wooshes in a tornado of destruction. The camera which the audience is watching from also begins to shake and move towards the mess.

The camera screen cracks. Title “bashful” flies from off screen as announcer’s voice over says the name of the game and which devices you can purchase the game.
