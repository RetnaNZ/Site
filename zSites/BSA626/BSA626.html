Introduction


For this project I will build a high quality intro cinematic for a game called "bashful".
Clean and focused on the character and what he does.

The mechanics of the game are to "push the characters buttons" via clicking the character enough to make it so annoyed that so it will burst out in anger and destroy all objects around it. More destruction to more points.

This inspiration for this game came from a few sources, the music video "The Verve - Bitter Sweet Symphony" the game "Burnout" and from "Elmo"

Treatment

bashful

Genre : stylised game cinematic.
Duration: 1min 30sec





Opening shot: mid-shot. Frame is pitch black, nothing is to be seen. (stage lights sound effect starts) white Spotlight lights center of frame with our character being blinded by the light (like the Mr Bean intro). Reacts like a possum in the headlights of a car. (the sound of more stage lights begin to click, like the batcave scene. *see reference board*) bright white LED panels light the entire room, which reveals nothing much but a blank room and the character.

Characters body language and facial expressions portray a shy/timid creature which does not like being “in the spotlight”. The character is confused about where it is and what is going on.

The character spots something off screen which the audience cannot see. A 3D computer cursor floats into the shot from right to left.

Character is curious as to what this strange thing is. Cautiously looking at the cursor, the cursor suddenly floats around the back of the character and clicks on its bottom. Character jumps and reacts with a slight hop. The character is still quite unsure about what just happened, but a little more cautiously this time looks towards the floating cursor.

As the cursor slowly starts to move again, the Character tries to slowly follow around with the cursor hoping not to be caught off guard like last time. Suddenly the mouse quickly whips around the back of the character and clicks on its bottom again. With another jump from the character.

Now the Character has some idea of what is going on and is beginning to become upset at the situation (shown both with body and face language). The audience should also get a clue about what is going on.

So now the duet has begun, the cursor starts to slowly “prowl” around the character. Almost like a western movie standoff. The cursor’s movement is too fast for our character and begins to click him over and over again, almost toying with him,

The Character does not like this one bit. The cursor starts to get really cheeky and starts to laugh (through body language), Suddenly there is a change in lighting as the character begins to growl, followed by shaking of his fur, followed by an angry expression and growing of his body size.

The camera slowly zooms out to show the size comparison between the mouse and character now. The character mammoths the size of the small cursor.

Our character has a big grin come across his face as he twists his body in anticipation of a big release of energy. *The cursor gulps*.

The Character unwinds and begins a devastating spin attack, as the floor begins to tear towards the center and the cursor wooshes in a tornado of destruction. The camera which the audience is watching from also begins to shake and move towards the mess.

The camera screen cracks. Title “bashful” flies from off screen as announcer’s voice over says the name of the game and which devices you can purchase the game.

The early stages of this project brought up a few very important questions. For example:

"What makes something cute?"

A study by Konrad Lorenz in the early 1940s found that the shape of an infant's head positively correlated with adult caregiving and an increased perception of "cute". ... The study concluded that a large head shape increased perceived cuteness, which then elicited a positive response in adult caretaking.

  • Change of head proportions (especially the relative size of the maxilla and mandible) as a function of age
  • Use of eyebrows is an effective way of making puppy dog eyes

How am I going to create enough expression from such a limited character?

This was a big one, there is both the look and feel of the character which is hard enough on it's own. There was also the question of the rig and how to acheive the type of movement that I am going for.

It has been a long time since I have played with rigging and "all of its wonderful features!"... Hahaha, no but after a few years of life experience, alot more things are clicking and I was able to pull off a pretty successful Squash and stretch rig setup.

I will need much more experimentation to see exactly what type of animation movement I am going for. Will keep you posted.
